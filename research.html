Research Experience

2021 – Present

Assistant Professor, Krea University, India.
At Krea, I am currently working on the following projects: computational modeling of pathological brain states; understanding patterns of comorbidity prevalence in brain trauma patients using the TBIMS database; understanding brain function across different socioeconomic groups; identification of biomarkers for psychiatric conditions from non-invasive physiological data (EEG, ECG).

2020 – 2021

Post-doctoral Researcher, Blue Brain Project, EPFL, Switzerland.
Worked on digital reconstruction of the mouse hippocampus under Prof. Henry Markram.

2016 – 2020

Post-doctoral Fellow, University of Michigan, USA.
Post-doctoral research fellow in Prof. Omar J. Ahmed’s laboratory, University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My research work was focused on studying pathological brain states (traumatic brain injury) using realistic computational modeling and understand their cellular and network level biophysical mechanisms in order to design novel therapeutics.

2011 – 2015

PhD Student, OIST, Japan.
Graduate student in Computational Neuroscience Unit, Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology, Graduate University (Okinawa, Japan), under the supervision of Prof. Erik De Schutter. My research work was focused on Computational modeling of information processing in the cerebellar cortex.

2009 – 2010

Master’s Researcher, University of Sheffield, UK.
Master’s research student in Prof. Kevin Gurney’s laboratory located at Department of Psychology, University of Sheffield, United Kingdom. My research was focused on constructing a two compartmental model of substantia nigra dopamine neurons.
